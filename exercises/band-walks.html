Band Walks & Monster Walks

Strength / Activation Beginner to Intermediate Month 2+

Overview

Band walks are functional exercises that challenge the hip abductors (especially glute medius) during movement. Unlike machine exercises, band walks require active stabilization and closely mimic the demands of walking, running, and athletic movements. They're a staple in ACL injury prevention and rehabilitation programs.

Key Benefits

  • Functional hip strength: Works muscles during movement
  • Glute medius activation: Key stabilizer for single-leg stance
  • Knee valgus prevention: Trains hip control over knee position
  • Minimal equipment: Just needs a resistance band
  • Warm-up or workout: Versatile programming
  • Multiple variations: Easy to progress or modify

Lateral Band Walk

Setup

  1. Place band around ankles (or above knees for easier version)
  2. Feet shoulder-width apart, slight tension in band
  3. Quarter squat position (slight knee bend)
  4. Chest up, core engaged

Execution

  1. Step sideways with leading leg
  2. Follow with trailing leg, maintaining band tension
  3. Don't let feet come together (keep tension)
  4. Stay low in quarter squat throughout
  5. Take 10-15 steps one direction
  6. Repeat in opposite direction

Key Form Points

  • Stay low: Maintain squat position throughout
  • Constant tension: Band should always be stretched
  • Knees out: Push against band, don't let knees cave
  • Control trailing leg: Don't let it snap back
  • Toes forward: Avoid turning feet out

Monster Walk (Forward/Backward)

Setup

  1. Band around ankles
  2. Wide stance, band under tension
  3. Quarter squat position
  4. Arms forward for balance

Forward Monster Walk

  1. Step forward and out at 45-degree angle with one leg
  2. Keep band stretched throughout
  3. Step forward and out with other leg
  4. Maintain wide stance and squat position
  5. Take 10-15 steps forward

Backward Monster Walk

  1. Same as forward but stepping back
  2. Step back and out at angle
  3. More challenging for glutes
  4. Take 10-15 steps backward

Variations & Progressions

Band Above Knees

Easier version. Good starting point. Less ankle/calf fatigue.

Band at Ankles

Standard progression. More challenging. Works full kinetic chain.

Band at Feet

Most challenging. Harder to maintain form. Advanced only.

Double Band

One band above knees, one at ankles. Maximum resistance.

X-Band Walk

  • Stand on band, cross it, hold handles at hips
  • Creates X pattern in front of body
  • Walk laterally or do monster walks
  • Adds upper body engagement

Sumo Walk

  • Extra wide stance, deep squat
  • Small lateral steps
  • Increases glute max involvement

Common Mistakes

Standing Upright

Losing the squat position. Stay low throughout the exercise.

Losing Band Tension

Feet coming too close together. Maintain resistance throughout.

Leaning Away

Tilting torso toward stepping leg. Keep torso vertical.

Toes Turning Out

Externally rotating to cheat. Keep toes pointed forward.

Knees Caving In

Letting knees collapse. Push out against the band.

Exercise Prescription

Exercise Volume Rest Use
Lateral Band Walk 2-3 x 15 steps each way 30-45s Warm-up or workout
Monster Walk (Forward) 2-3 x 15 steps 30-45s Workout
Monster Walk (Backward) 2-3 x 15 steps 30-45s Workout

Frequency: 2-3x per week or daily as activation/warm-up

Band Selection

  • Light band: Initial learning, warm-up, high reps
  • Medium band: Most common for workout use
  • Heavy band: Advanced, strength focus

Mini loop bands (small continuous loops) work best. Long resistance bands can also be tied in a loop.

Perfect Warm-Up

Band walks are excellent before any lower body workout or sport activity. 2 sets of lateral and monster walks activates the glutes and prepares the hips for more demanding movements.

Feel the Burn

You should feel these primarily in the side of your hip (glute medius area). If you feel them more in the front of the hip or thighs, focus on:

  • Keeping toes forward
  • Pushing knees out
  • Using a lighter band
