Barbell Hip Thrust

Strength / Glutes Intermediate to Advanced Month 4+

Overview

The barbell hip thrust is the gold standard for building glute strength. Unlike squats and deadlifts where glutes work alongside other muscles, the hip thrust isolates the glutes with maximum tension at hip lockout. Research shows it produces the highest glute activation of any exercise, making it essential for ACL rehabilitation and athletic performance.

Key Benefits

  • Maximum glute activation: Highest EMG of any glute exercise
  • Heavy loading: Can use significant weight safely
  • Low knee stress: Minimal loading through knee joint
  • Hip extension power: Critical for sprinting and jumping
  • Lockout strength: Builds end-range hip extension
  • Athletic carryover: Improves acceleration and power

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Sit on floor with upper back against bench (mid-scapula level)
  2. Roll barbell over legs to hip crease
  3. Use barbell pad for comfort
  4. Feet flat, about shoulder-width, shins vertical at top
  5. Grip bar wide for stability

Execution

  1. Drive through heels and squeeze glutes
  2. Lift hips until body is straight from shoulders to knees
  3. Posterior pelvic tilt at top (tuck tailbone slightly)
  4. Squeeze glutes hard at lockout for 1-2 seconds
  5. Lower with control, don't bounce
  6. Touch down briefly, then drive up again

Key Form Points

  • Chin tucked: Look forward, not up (prevents neck strain)
  • Posterior pelvic tilt: Prevents lower back hyperextension
  • Foot position: Shins should be vertical at top
  • Drive through heels: Minimizes hamstring involvement
  • Full lockout: Complete hip extension each rep

Setup Tips

Bench Height

  • Ideally bench hits at mid-scapula when seated
  • Standard bench height (17-18") works for most
  • Too high = neck strain, too low = limited ROM

Bar Pad

  • Essential for comfort with heavy weight
  • Hip thrust specific pads are best
  • Squat pad works as alternative
  • Folded mat in a pinch

Foot Placement

  • Feet closer = more quad
  • Feet farther = more hamstring
  • Middle position = glute focused
  • Adjust so shins are vertical at top

Progressions

Level 1: Floor Glute Bridge (Month 2)

  • No bench, just floor bridge
  • Master the squeeze at top
  • See glute bridge page for details

Level 2: Bodyweight Hip Thrust (Month 3)

  • Upper back on bench, no weight
  • Learn positioning and movement
  • 3 sets of 15-20 reps

Level 3: Light Barbell (Month 4)

  • Start with just the bar or light weight
  • Focus on technique
  • 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Level 4: Progressive Loading (Month 4+)

  • Add weight gradually
  • Can eventually go quite heavy
  • 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Level 5: Single-Leg Hip Thrust (Month 5+)

  • One leg at a time
  • Excellent for addressing asymmetry
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each leg

Why Hip Thrusts Excel

Research by Bret Contreras shows hip thrusts produce superior glute activation compared to squats and deadlifts. The horizontal force vector and peak tension at lockout make it uniquely effective.

Loading Potential

Athletes can often hip thrust 1.5-2x their back squat weight. The exercise allows heavy loading with relatively low injury risk. Progress patiently to build up.
