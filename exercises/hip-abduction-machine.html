Hip Abduction & Adduction Machine

Strength / Machine Beginner Month 2+

Overview

Hip abduction and adduction machines isolate the muscles that control lateral hip movement. The abductors (primarily glute medius) are crucial for single-leg stability and preventing knee valgus (inward collapse). The adductors help with stability and are often weak after ACL surgery due to compensatory movement patterns.

Key Benefits

  • Isolated strengthening: Targets specific hip muscles
  • Knee alignment: Strong abductors prevent knee valgus
  • Easy to use: Machine guides movement path
  • Adjustable resistance: Progress incrementally
  • Low knee stress: No loading through knee joint
  • Seated position: Comfortable for early rehab

Hip Abduction (Pushing Out)

Setup

  1. Sit with back flat against pad
  2. Place outer thighs against pads
  3. Adjust range limiter if available
  4. Select appropriate weight (start light)
  5. Grip handles for stability

Execution

  1. Push legs outward against pads
  2. Squeeze glutes at end range
  3. Hold 1-2 seconds at maximum separation
  4. Return to start with control
  5. Don't let weights slam together

Muscles Worked

  • Gluteus medius: Primary hip abductor
  • Gluteus minimus: Assists abduction
  • TFL (tensor fasciae latae): Hip flexor/abductor

Hip Adduction (Squeezing In)

Setup

  1. Sit with back against pad
  2. Place inner thighs against pads
  3. Legs start in spread position
  4. Select appropriate weight

Execution

  1. Squeeze legs together against pads
  2. Focus on inner thigh contraction
  3. Hold 1-2 seconds at end range
  4. Return to start with control
  5. Maintain constant tension

Muscles Worked

  • Adductor magnus: Largest adductor
  • Adductor longus: Upper inner thigh
  • Adductor brevis: Deep adductor
  • Gracilis: Also crosses knee joint

Progressions

Level 1: Light Weight, Full ROM (Month 2)

  • Focus on muscle activation
  • Full range of motion
  • 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Level 2: Moderate Weight (Month 3)

  • Increase resistance
  • Maintain control throughout
  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Level 3: Pause Reps (Month 3+)

  • 3-second hold at end range
  • Increases time under tension
  • 3 sets of 10 reps

Level 4: Single-Leg (Month 4+)

  • One leg at a time if machine allows
  • Address asymmetries
  • 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

Common Mistakes

Using Momentum

Swinging legs instead of controlled movement. Slow down and feel the muscles work.

Leaning Forward

Coming off the back pad. Stay seated firmly against the backrest.

Partial Range

Not going through full motion. Complete the full range for maximum benefit.

Too Heavy Too Soon

Sacrificing form for weight. These are accessory exercises—quality over quantity.

Exercise Prescription

Exercise Sets x Reps Rest Frequency
Hip Abduction 3x12-15 45-60s 2-3x/week
Hip Adduction 3x12-15 45-60s 2-3x/week

Note: Often done as a superset (back-to-back) for efficiency.

Why Abductors Matter for ACL

Weak hip abductors contribute to dynamic knee valgus—the inward collapse of the knee during landing and cutting. This position is a primary mechanism for ACL injury. Strengthening glute medius helps maintain proper knee alignment during athletic movements.
