Reverse Lunge

Strength / Single-Leg Beginner to Intermediate Month 2-3+

Overview

The reverse lunge involves stepping backward rather than forward. This simple change makes it significantly more knee-friendly because it reduces shear force on the front knee and makes it easier to keep the shin vertical. For ACL rehabilitation, the reverse lunge is often preferred over forward lunges early in recovery.

Why Reverse is Better for ACL Rehab

  • Front knee stays more stable (no forward momentum)
  • Easier to keep shin vertical (less knee shear)
  • Better eccentric control on the front leg
  • More glute engagement due to body mechanics
  • Natural deceleration pattern

Key Benefits

  • Knee-friendly: Less anterior shear force
  • Better control: Easier to maintain form
  • Glute emphasis: Greater glute activation than forward lunge
  • Single-leg strength: Addresses asymmetries
  • Deceleration training: Important for sport

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
  2. Hands on hips, at chest, or at sides
  3. Core engaged, chest up

Execution

  1. Step one foot backward (about 2-3 feet)
  2. Land on ball of back foot
  3. Lower straight down, bending both knees
  4. Back knee approaches floor
  5. Front shin stays vertical or close to it
  6. Drive through front heel to stand
  7. Bring back foot to starting position

Key Form Points

  • Vertical shin: Front knee stays over ankle, not past toes
  • Upright torso: Slight forward lean is OK, but don't collapse
  • Hip-width: Step back, not in a line
  • Front heel down: Drive through heel, not toes
  • Controlled step: Don't let back leg swing

Progressions

Level 1: Supported Reverse Lunge (Month 2)

  • Hold wall or chair for balance
  • Focus on pattern, not depth
  • 3 sets of 8 each leg

Level 2: Bodyweight Reverse Lunge (Month 2-3)

  • No support, full depth
  • Back knee nearly touches floor
  • 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

Level 3: Dumbbell Reverse Lunge (Month 3+)

  • Hold dumbbells at sides
  • Progress weight gradually
  • 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

Level 4: Deficit Reverse Lunge (Month 4+)

  • Front foot on small platform (4-6 inches)
  • Increases range of motion
  • Greater strength challenge
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each leg

Level 5: Barbell Reverse Lunge (Month 5+)

  • Barbell on back (back squat position)
  • Heavy loading for strength
  • 3-4 sets of 6-8 each leg

Pushing Off Back Leg

Using back leg to stand up. Drive through front heel only.

Narrow Stance

Feet in line like a tightrope. Maintain hip-width throughout.

Rushing

Moving too fast, losing control. Slow and controlled is better.

Reverse vs Forward

For ACL rehab, reverse lunges are usually preferred:

  • Less knee shear force
  • Easier to control
  • Better glute activation

Progress to forward lunges later when ready.
