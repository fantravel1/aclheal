TRX & Suspension Training

Strength / Stability Beginner to Advanced Week 4+

Overview

Suspension trainers (TRX, rings, straps) use your body weight and gravity as resistance. What makes them valuable for ACL rehab is the ability to precisely scale difficultyâ€”simply change your body angle to make exercises easier or harder. They also require constant core engagement and provide assistance for single-leg exercises.

Key Benefits

  • Scalable difficulty: Adjust angle to match current ability
  • Assisted single-leg work: Straps provide support for balance
  • Core integration: Every exercise requires trunk stability
  • Joint-friendly: Closed-chain, body weight movements
  • Portable: Use at gym, home, or travel
  • Functional: Multi-joint, real-world movement patterns

Lower Body Exercises

TRX Assisted Squat

Great Early Exercise

  1. Hold TRX handles at chest height, arms extended
  2. Walk feet forward so straps are angled back
  3. Sit back into squat, using straps for balance/support
  4. Keep chest up, knees tracking over toes
  5. Push through heels to stand
  6. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Why it works: Straps allow deeper squats with less knee stress. Pull gently to assist as needed.

TRX Single-Leg Squat (Pistol Assist)

  1. Hold handles, stand on surgical leg
  2. Extend opposite leg in front
  3. Lower into single-leg squat, using straps for balance
  4. Go as deep as comfortable with good form
  5. Use arms to assist standing as needed
  6. 3 sets of 8-10 each leg

Progression: Use less arm assistance over time until minimal support needed.

TRX Lunge

  1. Face away from anchor, one foot in both foot cradles
  2. Hop forward to create distance
  3. Lower back knee toward floor (suspended lunge)
  4. Keep front knee over ankle
  5. Drive through front heel to return
  6. 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

TRX Hamstring Curl

  1. Lie on back, heels in foot cradles
  2. Lift hips into bridge position
  3. Pull heels toward glutes (curling motion)
  4. Keep hips elevated throughout
  5. Extend legs and repeat
  6. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Hamstring graft note: Start with bilateral, progress to single leg as strength allows.

TRX Single-Leg Hamstring Curl

Advanced (Month 3+)

  1. Same setup as bilateral curl
  2. One heel in cradle, other leg extended up
  3. Perform curl on single leg
  4. Maintain level pelvis throughout
  5. 3 sets of 8-10 each leg

Hip & Glute Exercises

TRX Hip Drop

  1. Side-lying position, bottom foot in cradle
  2. Lift hips off ground (side plank with foot suspended)
  3. Lower and raise hips with control
  4. Targets hip abductors and obliques
  5. 3 sets of 10-12 each side

TRX Crossing Balance Lunge

  1. Hold handles for balance
  2. Stand on one leg
  3. Reach opposite leg behind and across body
  4. Perform curtsy lunge pattern
  5. Return to single-leg stance
  6. 3 sets of 10 each leg

Core & Upper Body (Active Recovery)

TRX Plank

  1. Feet in cradles, hands on floor in plank position
  2. Maintain straight line from head to heels
  3. Suspended feet add instability challenge
  4. Hold 20-45 seconds, 3 sets

TRX Pike

Advanced Core

  1. Start in TRX plank position
  2. Pike hips up toward ceiling, keeping legs straight
  3. Lower with control back to plank
  4. 3 sets of 8-12 reps

TRX Row

  1. Hold handles, lean back with arms extended
  2. Body in straight line, heels on ground
  3. Pull chest toward handles
  4. Squeeze shoulder blades together
  5. Lower with control
  6. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Difficulty: Walk feet forward to make easier, backward to make harder.

TRX Chest Press

  1. Face away from anchor, hold handles
  2. Lean forward in plank-like angle
  3. Lower chest between hands
  4. Press back to start
  5. 3 sets of 10-15 reps

Common Mistakes

Slack Straps

Letting straps go loose during exercise. Maintain tension throughout movement.

Wrong Length

Straps too long or short for exercise. Adjust length before starting each exercise.

Sagging Core

Letting hips drop during suspended exercises. Engage core before movement begins.

Relying Too Much on Arms

Over-assisting with upper body on leg exercises. Use minimal assistance for maximum benefit.

Scaling Difficulty

  • Easier: Stand more upright, use more arm assist
  • Harder: Increase body angle, minimize arm assist
  • Hardest: Single-leg, eyes closed, add movement

Setup Tips

  • Anchor above head height
  • Check stability before each use
  • Keep handles even length
  • Standard length: mid-calf when hanging
