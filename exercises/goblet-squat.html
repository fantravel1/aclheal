Goblet Squat

Strength / Squat Pattern Beginner to Intermediate Month 3+

Overview

The goblet squat is an excellent introduction to weighted squatting after ACL surgery. Holding a weight at chest level naturally promotes an upright torso, good depth, and proper mechanics. It's safer and easier to learn than barbell squats while still allowing significant loading for strength development.

Key Benefits

  • Natural form: Weight position encourages upright posture
  • Self-limiting: You can only hold so much—prevents overloading
  • Easy to learn: Simpler than back or front squat
  • Core activation: Holding weight engages anterior core
  • Depth promotion: Front load helps achieve full depth
  • Minimal equipment: One dumbbell or kettlebell

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Hold dumbbell vertically at chest, cupping one end
  2. Or hold kettlebell by horns against chest
  3. Feet shoulder-width or slightly wider
  4. Toes pointed slightly out (15-30 degrees)
  5. Elbows pointing down, weight tight to body

Execution

  1. Initiate by pushing hips back slightly
  2. Bend knees and lower body between legs
  3. Keep chest up and elbows inside knees
  4. Descend until thighs are parallel or lower
  5. Knees track over toes (push out if needed)
  6. Drive through whole foot to stand
  7. Squeeze glutes at top

Key Form Points

  • Chest up: Weight should help, not hinder this
  • Knees out: Don't let knees collapse inward
  • Heels down: Weight through whole foot, heels stay planted
  • Neutral spine: No excessive rounding or arching
  • Elbows inside: Elbows can push knees out at bottom

Progressions

Pre-Requisite: Bodyweight Squat

  • Master bodyweight squat with good depth first
  • Full ROM without pain or compensation
  • Usually Month 2-3 for most ACL patients

Level 1: Light Goblet Squat (Month 3)

  • 10-20 lb dumbbell or kettlebell
  • Focus on form and depth
  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Level 2: Moderate Load (Month 3-4)

  • 25-40 lb weight
  • Maintain perfect form
  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Level 3: Heavy Goblet (Month 4+)

  • 45-70+ lb (limited by holding ability)
  • Challenge strength while reinforcing form
  • 3-4 sets of 8-10 reps

Level 4: Tempo/Pause Variations

  • 3-second descent, 2-second pause at bottom
  • Increases time under tension
  • Builds strength and control
  • 3 sets of 8 reps

Level 5: Goblet to Barbell Transition

  • Once goblet becomes easy with heavy weight
  • Progress to front squat or back squat
  • Barbell allows continued loading

Variations

Goblet Box Squat

Squat to bench or box. Ensures consistent depth and teaches control at bottom.

Goblet Squat with Pulse

At bottom, small pulse up 2 inches and down before standing. Increases TUT.

1.5 Rep Goblet

Full squat, come halfway up, go back down, then stand. Double the work per rep.

Heels Elevated

Heels on small plates. Helps those with limited ankle mobility achieve depth.

Common Mistakes

Weight Drifting Forward

Letting weight pull away from chest. Keep it tight against body throughout.

Heels Rising

Coming up on toes, especially at bottom. Keep heels planted; elevate heels if needed.

Knee Valgus

Knees collapsing inward. Actively push knees out over toes.

Forward Lean

Torso falling forward excessively. The weight should help prevent this—keep chest up.

Partial Depth

Not reaching parallel or beyond. If mobility limits depth, work on ankle/hip mobility.

Exercise Prescription

Phase Load Sets x Reps Rest
Month 3 10-25 lbs 3x10-12 60s
Month 3-4 25-45 lbs 3x10-12 60-90s
Month 4+ 45-70+ lbs 3-4x8-10 90s

Frequency: 2x per week as part of lower body training

Dumbbell vs. Kettlebell

Both work well for goblet squats:

  • Dumbbell: Hold vertically, cupping one end. More common in gyms, easier to grip heavy.
  • Kettlebell: Hold by horns (handle sides). More natural hand position, slightly more compact.

Use whichever is available or feels more comfortable. The movement is the same.

Why Goblet First?

The front-loaded weight automatically corrects common squat faults:

  • Keeps torso upright
  • Counterbalances for depth
  • Engages core naturally
  • Self-limiting load

When to Progress

Move to barbell squats when:

  • Goblet form is excellent
  • Current weight feels easy
  • Grip becomes limiting factor
  • Usually month 4-5+
