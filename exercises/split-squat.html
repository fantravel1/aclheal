Split Squat

Strength / Single-Leg Beginner to Intermediate Month 2+

Overview

The split squat is a stationary single-leg exercise where you maintain a staggered stance throughout the movement. Unlike lunges, your feet don't move—you simply lower and raise your body. This makes it easier to control and an excellent progression before dynamic lunges and Bulgarian split squats.

Key Benefits

  • Single-leg focus: Addresses asymmetries between legs
  • Stable base: Feet stay planted—easier than lunges
  • Quad and glute: Primary movers for ACL rehab
  • Balance training: Challenges stability without dynamic movement
  • Low equipment: Bodyweight or add dumbbells
  • Progressive: Easy to scale difficulty

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Stand in staggered stance, one foot forward, one back
  2. Feet hip-width apart (not in line)
  3. Front foot flat, back foot on ball of foot
  4. Torso upright, core engaged
  5. Hands on hips or at sides

Execution

  1. Lower body straight down by bending both knees
  2. Back knee approaches floor (don't slam it)
  3. Front knee tracks over toes, doesn't cave in
  4. Keep torso upright throughout
  5. Drive through front foot to stand
  6. Complete all reps, then switch legs

Key Form Points

  • Vertical torso: Don't lean forward
  • Knee alignment: Front knee over 2nd-3rd toe
  • Hip-width stance: Not on a tightrope
  • Controlled descent: Don't drop down
  • Front leg focus: 80% of work should be front leg

Progressions

Level 1: Supported Split Squat (Month 2)

  • Hand on wall or chair for balance
  • Partial range of motion
  • Focus on alignment
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each leg

Level 2: Bodyweight Split Squat (Month 2-3)

  • No support, full range
  • Back knee nearly touches floor
  • 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

Level 3: Dumbbell Split Squat (Month 3+)

  • Hold dumbbells at sides
  • Progress weight gradually
  • 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

Level 4: Front Foot Elevated (Month 3+)

  • Front foot on small platform (2-4 inches)
  • Increases range of motion
  • Greater quad stretch at bottom
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each leg

Level 5: Bulgarian Split Squat

  • Rear foot elevated on bench
  • Much more challenging
  • See dedicated page for details

Common Mistakes

Feet in Line

Stance too narrow (like walking a tightrope). Keep feet hip-width for stability.

Knee Valgus

Front knee collapsing inward. Actively push knee out over toes.

Forward Lean

Torso tipping forward. Stay upright, weight centered.

Back Leg Dominant

Pushing too much from back leg. Focus drive through front heel.

Split Squat vs Lunge

  • Split squat: Feet stay planted, static
  • Lunge: Step forward or back each rep

Split squats are easier to control, making them ideal for early single-leg work.
