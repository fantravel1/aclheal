Lateral Movement Exercises

Agility / Strength Intermediate to Advanced Month 3+

Overview

Most ACL injuries occur during lateral movements—cutting, pivoting, side-stepping. Restoring comfort and competence with side-to-side movements is critical for return to sport. These exercises progressively build lateral strength, stability, and movement confidence.

Why Lateral Training Matters

The ACL primarily resists rotation and anterior translation. Lateral movements stress the knee differently than straight-line running. Training these patterns specifically is essential for preventing re-injury.

Key Benefits

  • Hip abductor strength: Controls knee position during lateral cuts
  • Lateral stability: Builds side-to-side control
  • Cutting preparation: Foundation for sport-specific movements
  • Balance challenge: Single-leg stance in motion
  • Confidence building: Comfort with lateral forces

Lateral Strength Exercises

Lateral Lunge

  1. Stand with feet together
  2. Step wide to one side
  3. Sit back into lunging leg (like single-leg squat)
  4. Keep stepping foot flat, knee tracking over toes
  5. Push off to return to start
  6. 3 sets of 10 each direction

Depth: Start shallow, progress deeper as strength improves.

Lateral Step-Down

  1. Stand on low step/box sideways (surgical leg on step)
  2. Lower opposite foot toward floor
  3. Touch heel lightly, then return
  4. Control with surgical leg (don't drop)
  5. 3 sets of 12 each leg

Cossack Squat

Advanced Lateral Lunge

  1. Wide stance, feet parallel
  2. Shift weight to one side, squatting deep
  3. Opposite leg stays straight, foot flat or heel up
  4. Push through heel to shift to other side
  5. 3 sets of 6-8 each side

Lateral Movement Drills

Side Shuffle (Month 3)

  1. Athletic stance: knees bent, hips back
  2. Step laterally with lead foot
  3. Follow with trail foot (don't cross feet)
  4. Stay low throughout movement
  5. 10-15 yards each direction, 3 sets

Cue: "Stay low, push off, don't bounce up."

Lateral Bounds (Month 4+)

  1. Stand on one leg
  2. Push off laterally, jumping to opposite leg
  3. Stick the landing for 2 seconds
  4. Bound back to starting side
  5. Start small (12-18 inches), progress wider
  6. 3 sets of 6-8 each direction

Skater Hops (Month 5+)

  1. Similar to lateral bounds but continuous
  2. Bound side to side without pause
  3. Swing arms across body for momentum
  4. Land softly, control each landing
  5. 20-30 seconds continuous, 3 sets

Lateral Shuffle with Direction Change

  1. Shuffle 5 yards in one direction
  2. Plant outside foot, change direction
  3. Shuffle back 5 yards
  4. Focus on controlled deceleration
  5. 3 sets of 6 direction changes

Cutting Progressions

Cutting vs. Pivoting

  • Sidestep cut: Plant outside foot, push off at angle (45-90°)
  • Crossover cut: Plant outside foot, cross inside leg in front
  • Pivot: Full 180° rotation on planted foot

Anticipated Cuts (Month 5+)

  1. Jog forward at 50% speed
  2. At cone, perform pre-planned 45° cut
  3. Know exactly when and where you'll cut
  4. Focus on technique: plant, push, accelerate
  5. Progress: 60% → 70% → 80% speed

Reactive Cuts (Month 6+)

  1. Partner points direction as you approach
  2. React and cut in indicated direction
  3. Start at 50% speed, progress to 80%+
  4. Unknown direction adds cognitive demand

Sport-Specific Cutting (Month 7+)

  • Cutting while dribbling (soccer, basketball)
  • Defensive slides with changes
  • Reaction to ball or opponent movement
  • Game-like scenarios at increasing intensity

Common Mistakes

Knee Valgus

Knee collapsing inward during lateral movements. Focus on "knee out" and hip strength.

Too Upright

Standing tall during shuffles/cuts. Stay low with hips back for power and control.

Crossing Feet

Feet crossing during side shuffle. Keep feet apart, lead with outside foot.

Skipping Progressions

Going straight to reactive cuts. Master anticipated movements first.
