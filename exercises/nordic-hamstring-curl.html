Nordic Hamstring Curl

Strength / Eccentric Advanced Month 4+

Overview

The Nordic hamstring curl is one of the most researched exercises for hamstring injury prevention. It emphasizes the eccentric (lengthening) phase of hamstring contraction, which is when most hamstring strains occur. Strong eccentric hamstring capacity also protects the ACL by resisting anterior tibial translation.

Research Evidence

Studies show Nordic hamstring training can reduce hamstring injury rates by up to 51% and ACL injury risk by approximately 50% when combined with other neuromuscular training. The exercise builds fascicle length and eccentric strength.

Key Benefits

  • Eccentric strength: Builds capacity to control lengthening
  • Injury prevention: Reduces hamstring strain risk
  • ACL protection: Hamstrings help stabilize the knee
  • Fascicle lengthening: Increases muscle fascicle length
  • No equipment needed: Partner or anchor point only

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Kneel on padded surface (use mat or towel)
  2. Have partner hold your ankles firmly, or hook feet under secure object
  3. Start with torso upright, hips extended
  4. Arms ready in front to catch yourself

Execution

  1. Keep hips extended (no hip flexion/bending at waist)
  2. Lower body toward ground slowly using hamstrings to resist
  3. Maintain straight line from knees through hips to shoulders
  4. Control descent as long as possible (3-5 seconds)
  5. When you can no longer control, catch yourself with hands
  6. Push back up with hands to return to start

Key Form Points

  • No hip hinge: Don't bend at waist—stay straight
  • Control is the goal: Fight the descent, don't free-fall
  • Use hands: Catch yourself—this is expected
  • Knee padding: Essential for comfort

Progressions

Level 1: Assisted (Month 4)

  • Use resistance band anchored in front
  • Band assists on the way down and up
  • Focus on learning the movement
  • 3 sets of 5-6 reps

Level 2: Partial Range (Month 4-5)

  • Lower only 1/3 of the way down
  • Control the limited range fully
  • Push back up with hands
  • 3 sets of 4-6 reps

Level 3: Half Range (Month 5-6)

  • Lower halfway down with control
  • Catch with hands, push back
  • 3 sets of 4-6 reps

Level 4: Full Nordic (Month 6+)

  • Lower as far as possible with control
  • May reach near floor before catching
  • Push back up with hands
  • 3 sets of 4-6 reps

Level 5: Concentric Return (Advanced)

  • Full control on the way down
  • Use hamstrings to pull back up (minimal hand assistance)
  • Very few athletes achieve full concentric Nordic
  • 2-3 sets of 3-5 reps

Variations & Equipment

Partner Assisted

Partner holds ankles and can provide light support at hips during descent.

Nordic Bench

Purpose-built bench with ankle pads. Allows solo training.

Lat Pulldown Machine

Kneel facing away, ankles under knee pad. Common gym modification.

Loaded Barbell

Anchor feet under heavy barbell. Ensure bar can't roll.

Common Mistakes

Hip Flexion (Bending)

Breaking at hips makes it easier but defeats purpose. Stay rigid from knees to shoulders.

Falling Too Fast

Free-falling without control. The eccentric is the exercise—fight it.

Too Much Too Soon

Attempting full range before ready. Progress through levels—DOMs can be severe.

Inadequate Ankle Support

Ankles slipping during movement. Ensure secure anchor.

Hamstring Graft Considerations

If you had a hamstring autograft, discuss Nordic curls with your surgeon or PT. Some protocols delay intensive eccentric hamstring work until month 5-6 to protect the graft harvest site. Start conservatively and monitor for discomfort at the harvest site (medial knee/upper tibia area).

Exercise Prescription

Phase Progression Volume Frequency
Introduction Band-assisted / Partial 3x5 1x/week
Building Half range 3x6 1-2x/week
Maintenance Full range 3x6-8 1-2x/week

Note: DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness) can be significant initially. Start with low volume and progress gradually. Allow 3-4 days between sessions early on.

Why Eccentric Matters

Most hamstring strains happen during eccentric contraction (muscle lengthening under load), like during late swing phase of running. Nordic curls specifically train this capacity.

Programming Tips

  • Start with 1x/week
  • Expect significant DOMS initially
  • Don't do before matches/hard sessions
  • 2-3 days recovery between sessions
