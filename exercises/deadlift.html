Deadlift

Strength / Hip Hinge Intermediate to Advanced Month 4+

Overview

The deadlift is a fundamental hip hinge movement that develops the entire posterior chain—glutes, hamstrings, and back. After ACL surgery, deadlifts help restore functional strength for lifting, athletic movements, and daily activities. The movement is relatively knee-friendly as primary motion occurs at the hip, though proper form is essential.

Key Benefits

  • Posterior chain strength: Glutes, hamstrings, and back
  • Functional movement: Picking things up from the ground
  • Hip-dominant: Less knee stress than squats
  • Total body strength: Grip, core, legs all engaged
  • Athletic transfer: Power development for sport
  • Highly loadable: Can progress to heavy weights

Conventional Deadlift

Setup

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart, barbell over mid-foot
  2. Bend at hips and knees to grip bar just outside legs
  3. Arms straight, shoulders over or slightly in front of bar
  4. Back flat, chest up, neck neutral
  5. Take slack out of bar (pull until bar touches plates)
  6. Brace core tightly

Execution

  1. Drive through whole foot while pushing floor away
  2. Keep bar close to body (dragging up legs)
  3. Extend hips and knees simultaneously
  4. Stand tall at top, squeeze glutes
  5. Don't lean back excessively at lockout
  6. Lower with control, hinging at hips first

Key Form Points

  • Flat back: No rounding of lower back
  • Bar path: Straight up, close to body
  • Hip hinge: Primary motion at hips, not knees
  • Bracing: Big breath, tight core throughout
  • Head position: Neutral, eyes forward or slightly down

Deadlift Variations

Trap Bar / Hex Bar Deadlift

Recommended for ACL Rehab

  • Stand inside hexagonal bar, grip handles at sides
  • More neutral spine position
  • Reduced lower back stress
  • More quad involvement than conventional
  • Excellent for ACL rehab—often first deadlift variation introduced

Sumo Deadlift

  • Wide stance, toes pointed out
  • Grip bar between legs
  • More upright torso
  • More hip/adductor emphasis
  • May feel better for some body types

Block/Rack Pulls

  • Bar elevated on blocks or rack pins
  • Reduces range of motion
  • Good for building lockout strength
  • Can be helpful early in return to deadlifting

Deficit Deadlift

  • Stand on small platform (1-3 inches)
  • Increases range of motion
  • More challenging off the floor
  • Advanced variation

Progressions

Pre-Requisite: Romanian Deadlift

  • Master hip hinge pattern first
  • RDL teaches proper hip mechanics
  • Usually Month 3-4

Level 1: Trap Bar (Month 4)

  • High handles if available
  • Light to moderate weight
  • Focus on hip hinge pattern
  • 3 sets of 8-10 reps

Level 2: Conventional Light (Month 4-5)

  • Standard barbell from floor
  • Conservative loading
  • Perfect form before adding weight
  • 3 sets of 6-8 reps

Level 3: Progressive Loading (Month 5+)

  • Gradual weight increases
  • Can work in lower rep ranges
  • Monitor for any knee symptoms
  • 3-5 sets of 3-6 reps

Common Mistakes

Rounded Lower Back

Back rounding under load. Reduce weight, focus on bracing and flat back.

Bar Drifting Forward

Bar moving away from body. Keep bar dragging up shins and thighs.

Hips Rising First

Hips shooting up before bar moves. Drive legs and extend hips together.

Hyperextending at Top

Leaning back excessively. Stand tall with neutral spine at lockout.

Jerking the Bar

Yanking bar off floor. Take slack out first, then drive smoothly.

Exercise Prescription

Phase Variation Sets x Reps Load
Month 4 Trap bar / Light conventional 3x8-10 Light-Moderate
Month 5 Conventional 3x6-8 Moderate
Month 6+ Conventional / Sumo 3-5x3-6 Progressive

Frequency: 1-2x per week

Note: Deadlifts are fatiguing. Allow adequate recovery between sessions.

Trap Bar Advantage

For ACL patients, the trap bar deadlift is often preferable early on:

  • More neutral spine position
  • Easier to learn
  • Less lower back stress
  • High handles reduce ROM if needed

Deadlift vs. Squat

Both are valuable but different:

  • Deadlift: Hip-dominant, more posterior chain
  • Squat: Knee-dominant, more quad

Include both in your program for complete development.
