Lateral Lunge

Strength / Frontal Plane Intermediate Month 3+

Overview

The lateral lunge (side lunge) trains movement in the frontal planeâ€”stepping sideways. Most ACL injuries occur during lateral cutting and pivoting, making frontal plane strength essential for return to sport. This exercise also develops hip mobility and adductor flexibility while building single-leg strength.

Key Benefits

  • Frontal plane strength: Essential for cutting and lateral movements
  • Hip mobility: Opens hips in abduction
  • Adductor stretch: Lengthens inner thigh under load
  • Single-leg loading: Builds unilateral strength
  • Athletic carryover: Mimics sport movements
  • Balance challenge: Multi-planar stability

How to Perform

Setup

  1. Stand with feet together or hip-width
  2. Hands at chest or on hips
  3. Core engaged, chest up

Execution

  1. Step wide to one side (2-3 feet)
  2. Push hips back, bend stepping leg
  3. Keep trailing leg straight
  4. Both feet point forward
  5. Lower until thigh is near parallel
  6. Push through bent leg to return to start
  7. Alternate sides or complete all reps on one side

Key Form Points

  • Hips back: This is a hip hinge, not just a knee bend
  • Knee tracks toes: Don't let knee cave inward
  • Toes forward: Both feet point straight ahead
  • Straight trail leg: Keep non-working leg extended
  • Chest up: Maintain upright torso

Progressions

Level 1: Lateral Shift (Month 2-3)

  • Wide stance, don't step
  • Shift weight side to side
  • Learn the hip hinge pattern
  • 3 sets of 10 each side

Level 2: Bodyweight Lateral Lunge (Month 3)

  • Full stepping motion
  • Controlled depth
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each side

Level 3: Goblet Lateral Lunge (Month 3-4)

  • Hold dumbbell or kettlebell at chest
  • Weight helps counterbalance
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each side

Level 4: Dumbbell Lateral Lunge (Month 4+)

  • Dumbbells at sides or shoulders
  • Progressive loading
  • 3 sets of 8-10 each side

Level 5: Lateral Lunge to Balance (Month 4+)

  • Push off and balance on single leg
  • Don't set foot down between reps
  • Adds proprioceptive challenge
  • 3 sets of 6-8 each side

Variations

Cossack Squat

Go as deep as mobility allows, heel may lift on straight leg. Maximum hip mobility.

Slider Lateral Lunge

Trailing foot on slider. Smooth, controlled movement.

Deficit Lateral Lunge

Step down onto lower surface. Increases range.

Curtsy Lunge

Step behind and across. Different angle, more glute med.

Common Mistakes

Knee Caving

Working knee collapsing inward. Push knee out over toes.

Toes Turning Out

Feet pointing outward instead of forward. Keep toes straight.

No Hip Hinge

Just bending knee without sitting back. Push hips back.

Insufficient Step

Step too short limits ROM. Take a wide step.

Why Frontal Plane Matters

Most gym exercises are sagittal (forward/back). But sports happen in all planes. ACL injuries often occur in the frontal plane during cutting. Train laterally to protect your knee.
