Seated Calf Raise

Strength / Lower Leg Beginner Month 2+

Overview

The seated calf raise targets the soleus muscle, which lies beneath the larger gastrocnemius. Because the knee is bent during this exercise, the gastrocnemius is shortened and less active, allowing the soleus to do most of the work. Complete calf development requires both seated (soleus) and standing (gastrocnemius) calf raises.

Soleus vs. Gastrocnemius

  • Gastrocnemius: Two-headed muscle that crosses both knee and ankle. Works with straight knee (standing calf raise).
  • Soleus: Deeper muscle, only crosses ankle. Works with bent knee (seated calf raise).

Both are important for walking, running, and jumping. Train both for complete development.

Key Benefits

  • Soleus isolation: Targets often-neglected muscle
  • Posture support: Soleus helps with standing balance
  • Low knee stress: No loading through the knee
  • Running support: Soleus is key for push-off
  • Easy to load: Can use significant weight

How to Perform

Machine Setup

  1. Sit in seated calf raise machine
  2. Place balls of feet on platform, heels hanging off
  3. Adjust knee pad to sit snugly on lower thighs
  4. Release safety and let heels drop (stretch position)

Execution

  1. Push through balls of feet to raise heels
  2. Rise as high as possible (full plantarflexion)
  3. Squeeze at the top for 1-2 seconds
  4. Lower slowly with control
  5. Allow full stretch at bottom before next rep

No Machine Alternative

  1. Sit on bench with feet on elevated surface
  2. Place dumbbell or weight plate on knees

  4. Use folded towel under weight for comfort

Key Form Points

  • Full range: Deep stretch to full contraction
  • Controlled tempo: Don't bounce at bottom
  • Even pressure: Both feet working equally
  • No rocking: Movement only at ankle

Progressions

Level 1: Light Weight (Month 2)

  • Focus on full range of motion
  • Establish mind-muscle connection
  • 3 sets of 15-20 reps

Level 2: Moderate Weight (Month 3)

  • Increase resistance
  • Maintain full ROM
  • 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Level 3: Heavy Loading (Month 4+)

  • Progressive weight increases
  • Can go quite heavy on this exercise
  • 3-4 sets of 10-15 reps

Level 4: Single-Leg (Month 4+)

  • One leg at a time
  • Addresses asymmetry
  • 3 sets of 10-12 each leg

Common Mistakes

Bouncing

Using momentum at bottom. Pause briefly in stretched position.

Partial Range

Not going through full ROM. Full stretch and full squeeze each rep.

Rushing

Moving too fast. Calves respond well to time under tension.

Leaning Forward

Shifting weight to reduce difficulty. Stay upright.

Frequency: 2-3x per week

Why Both Calf Exercises?

The gastrocnemius (standing raise) is faster-twitch and important for explosive push-off. The soleus (seated raise) is more endurance-oriented and supports posture. Train both for complete development.
